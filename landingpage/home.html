Story: Silliest thing that's happened to me since moving to Chicago

I'm super hungry and on the UChicago downtown campus looking for cheap places nearby. I find a place with tons of reviews talking about how amazing the "vibes" are. It's a 6-minute walk and seems cheap, so I might as well try it. I go in and get shown to my seat, but the server is weirdly pushy, like he's trying to start an argument. My initial reaction is, "Everyone has off days, I guess." I order my food, and the server comes back after a while and does the same thing again, except this time he seems confused too. He presents a paper hat with the diner logo on it. This does not help. He says I'm supposed to wear the hat. "Either it is a peace offering of some kind," I think, "or it is a mysterious birthday." I'm too lost to question anything, but I am nothing if not determined. I try the hat on to see if proximity to my brain was the root cause of the confusion, but it doesn't really sit right because of my hair. I tell him it doesn't fit, and he says, "Try harder." He looks at me and notices I'm really confused. Then he says, "You know this is a novelty diner, right?" In the same moment we both realized I was not aware people came here to be made fun of as entertainment. There was a beat of silence. We were bugs trapped in the amber of social awkwardness, caught in a moment of stillness and self-reflection. I suddenly master stoicism. In mere seconds, I have reached enlightenment. The moment ends, and he wilts in slow motion. I asked him if he would like me to wear the hat, and there was absolute sincerity in his voice when he tells me "I would feel much more comfortable if you did." However, as the event had not fully balded my head, the hat still does not fit. For the rest of my time there, I hear him loudly making fun of the other people in the place, then getting to my table and instantly switching to being as genial as possible. I tipped 100% and have not returned.

Where I grew up

I grew up in New Jersey in the Somerset area. My family moved around twice growing up but I end up spending most of my adult life in Hillsborough (a surburban area). Even though I went to a few different schools during my childhood, the one that shaped my life the most was TEECS; please reach out to me if you are a fellow alumni!

My favorite local spot

Now that I'm in Chicago, one of my favorite places to hang out is the Midwest Buddhist temple in Lincoln Park. Wonderful people, beautiful ornamentation, and so much history to discover.

My perfect Sunday

I love to spend Sunday afternoons reading. I read everything from pure fiction to psycology to creative nonfiction about the modern world's dependence on illegal sand mining. My favorite book of all time is Mother of Learning by Domagoj Kurmaic, which is an incredible magic/sci-fi/groundhog-day blend. If any of that even slightly interests you, I would highly recommend the book.

My love for spicy food

My parents were from India, so I have a loving relationship with spicy foods of all kinds. My grandfather was such an avid enjoyer of spicy foods that he used to eat green chilies like they were grapes. I am not so far along that path, but I enjoy a good Thai curry.

Playing musical instruments

I love playing the piano, I've been playing since I was 7. During my undergrad I even started a band. After moving to Chicago, I've unfortunately had fewer opportunities to play. I am always looking for other music lovers; reach out if you are looking for someone on keys!
